Overview

Australian Migration Statistics, 2024–25 — Released November 2025.

Australian Migration Statistics is produced as an accompaniment to the publication Australia's Migration Trends. Detailed tables are provided summarising migration activity for the financial year including 10-year comparative data. Coverage includes permanent and temporary migration, citizenship, Net Overseas Migration and labour market tables.

Report period and frequency: 2024–25 financial year, published annually.

Report contents

Tables in this package cover: Australia's permanent migration outcome (1.0–1.18), temporary entry visas (2.0–2.4), Humanitarian Program (3.0–3.4), visa non-compliance (4.0–4.4), Net Overseas Migration (5.0–5.1), citizenship (6.0), and migration and the labour market (7.0–7.8). Detailed statistical information is available at data.gov.au and the Department of Home Affairs website (Research and statistics).

Data Items and Terminology

Applicant Type
Under the Migration Regulations 1994 there are two types: primary applicants and secondary applicants. Where a table or graph heading includes the word 'primary', dependants (secondary applicants) are not included.
Citizenship
The country of citizenship of the visa applicant or visa holder. Where a person has more than one citizenship, either the citizenship of the travel document or that nominated by the applicant is used.
Client Location
The location at the time the application was lodged: In Australia (onshore) or Outside Australia (offshore).
Humanitarian Program
Comprised of an offshore resettlement component and an onshore protection component; provides protection to refugees and resettlement in line with Australia's international obligations.
Migration Program
The primary pathway to Australian permanent residence. It has three streams: (1) Skill stream, (2) Family stream, (3) Special Eligibility.
Net Overseas Migration (NOM)
Net gain or loss of population through people arriving in and departing from Australia, based on duration of stay in or out of Australia for 12 months or more.
Outcome
In Migration Program reporting, outcomes are the number of visas granted (net of certain cancellations and exclusions).
Primary Applicant
The person who must satisfy the primary criteria for the grant of a visa (principal applicant).
Secondary Applicant
Must satisfy secondary criteria; generally a dependant of the primary applicant.
